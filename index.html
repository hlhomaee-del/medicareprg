RAC, MIP, HEAT & CMS IOM

These four programs all protect Medicare's financial integrity — but each has a distinct job. Think of them as a team:

🔍
RAC
Recovery Audit Contractor
Private auditors who find and recover improper Medicare payments — both overpayments and underpayments.
🛡️
MIP
Medicare Integrity Program
Legal authority (Section 1893 of the SSA) for CMS to contract out integrity work like audits and education.
⚖️
HEAT
Fraud Enforcement Team
Joint DOJ + HHS task force that investigates and prosecutes criminal healthcare fraud.
📘
CMS IOM
CMS Internet Only Manual
The official operating manual giving Medicare contractors claims processing and coverage guidance.

Conditions of Participation & Deeming

Conditions of Participation (CoPs) are the minimum health and safety standards every facility must meet to participate in Medicare/Medicaid.

Deeming happens when CMS recognizes a private accreditor whose standards meet or exceed CoPs — accredited facilities are then "deemed" compliant without a separate state survey.

🏛️
CMS
Sets CoPs
→
🏅
Accreditor
Standards ≥ CoPs
→
🏥
Facility
Deemed compliant
Key point: Deeming never lowers the bar. The accreditor's standards must match or exceed CMS CoPs. CMS can still do validation surveys on deemed facilities.

HIPAA Transactions & Code Sets

HIPAA's Administrative Simplification mandates standard electronic formats (ASC X12N transactions) for all billing activities, replacing the old payer-specific paper and flat-file formats.
